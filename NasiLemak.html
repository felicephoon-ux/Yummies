Nasi Lemak is the national dish in Malaysia and beloved by all local Malaysians.
You definitely must try this when you are in Malaysia! Basically, it is rice cooked in coconut milk, topped with spicy sambal, fried crispy anchovies, toasted peanuts, cucumber slices, and a boiled or fried egg.

üìú History of Nasi Lemak

Nasi lemak was mentioned as early as 1875 in a French‚ÄìMalay dictionary compiled by Pierre-Etienne-Lazare Favre, a Roman Catholic missionary based on the Malay peninsula, and later in The Circumstances of Malay Life by Richard Olaf Winstedt in 1909. With roots in Malay culture and cuisine, the name of the dish in Malay literally means "fat rice", but in this context, it signifies "rich" or "creamy". This comes from the cooking method of soaking rice in coconut cream and steaming it with pandan leaves, which give the rice its distinct aroma and flavor.

Traditionally, nasi lemak is wrapped and served in banana leaves, accompanied by a hot spicy sauce (sambal) and garnishes like fresh cucumber slices, fried anchovies (ikan bilis), roasted peanuts, and hard-boiled or fried eggs. These simple yet flavorful accompaniments are what make nasi lemak Malaysia‚Äôs most iconic national dish.
