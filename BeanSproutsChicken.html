Homemade Recipe

While nothing beats enjoying this dish in Ipoh itself, here's how you can create a delicious version at home:

Ingredients

  • 1 whole free-range chicken (about 1.5kg)
  • 400g fresh bean sprouts
  • 2 inches ginger, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 2 spring onions, cut into sections
  • 3 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1 tbsp fried shallot oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Water for poaching

Preparation Steps

  1. Clean the chicken and pat it dry. Rub with salt and let it sit for 15 minutes.
  2. In a large pot, bring enough water to cover the chicken to a boil. Add ginger, garlic, and spring onions.
  3. Gently lower the chicken into the boiling water. Return to a boil, then reduce heat to a gentle simmer.
  4. Cook for about 30-40 minutes (depending on size), until the chicken is cooked through but still tender.
  5. Remove the chicken and immediately plunge into ice water to stop the cooking process. This keeps the skin firm and the meat juicy.
  6. Strain and reserve the chicken broth for later use.
  7. Blanch the bean sprouts in the reserved boiling broth for just 30 seconds. Drain immediately.
  8. Combine the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar, and sesame oil in a small bowl and mix well.
  9. Chop the chicken into serving pieces. Arrange on a plate with bean sprouts.
  10. Drizzle the sauce over the chicken and bean sprouts. Garnish with fried shallots and sliced spring onions.

Chef's Tip: For authentic Ipoh-style bean sprouts, try to find thicker, crunchier varieties. The secret is in quick blanching to maintain their crisp texture.
